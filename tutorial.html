Choosing a DAW

Choosing a DAW (Digital Audio Workstation) is something that does not affect your journey as a producer as much as beginners think. Still, many producers spend a lot of time thinking about what software to use. For a beginner, suitable choices are:

  1. FL Studio
  2. BandLab
  3. Ableton
  4. GarageBand

Using Samples and Loops Is Not a Crime

Many producers think that from the very start they need to make everything by themselves, which is wrong. You can start by using loops as it will help you save time and help you develop other skills such as drum placement. You can get to making your own melodies later.

So the next time you hear someone say "well, I make all my melodies by myself" just say sybau nerd.

Don't Switch

After choosing a DAW, don't start thinking that switching DAWs will make you better. Every DAW has similar features. Focus on cooking, not switching kitchens.

Do you need a nice setup to start?
No. Start with what you have. It's enough. Don't waste your money making excuses like "I can't make songs without a setup." Kendrick made Section.80 in his tour bus.
